The outpatient control gap

Current skin-lesion treatment decisions balance clearance, cosmetic outcome, side effects, workflow, and follow-up burden. Laser therapy is promising, but broader adoption needs better real-time feedback and reproducible dose control.

🔥

Over-treatment risk

Too much thermal spread can injure healthy tissue, worsening scarring, pigment changes, or delayed healing.

🎯

Under-treatment risk

Too little coverage can leave viable lesion tissue behind, increasing the chance of repeat procedures.

🧭

Workflow uncertainty

Clinicians need practical tools that fit outpatient care without requiring hospital-scale imaging infrastructure.

A control layer for laser therapy

Noema-Laser does not replace the clinician. It gives clinicians real-time thermal intelligence before and during dose delivery.

Real-time thermal sensing

Patient-specific digital twin

Safety-constrained AI control

The controller selects a dose intended to cover the lesion while respecting healthy-tissue safety thresholds.
